FOR THE SECOND SENATORIAL DISTRICT
To appropriate Forty Two Thousand Dollars ($42,000.00) from
revenues collected from poker fees in the Second Senatorial District;
and for other purposes.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE SECOND SENATORIAL DISTRICT
DELEGATION PURSUANT TO CHAPTER 4, DIVISION 1, TITLE 1 OF
THE COMMONWEALTH CODE:
1 SECTION 1. PURPOSE.
2 The purpose of this Act is to appropriate Forty Two Thousand Dollars
3 ($42,000.00 USD) from revenues collected from poker fees in the Second
4 Senatorial District. The certification of funds was prepared on August 25, 2025 by
5 the Resident Department Head of the Department of Finance, Tinian. This
6 appropriation of local funds is not part of the FY 2025 Budget Act. Therefore, this
7 local appropriation of local funds does not contravene 1 CMC §7401 and 1 CMC
8 §7404(d) or any other provision of the CNMI Budget Act.
9 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATIONS.
HOUSE LOCAL BILL No. 24- 2g
1 Notwithstanding any provision(s) of law, statutes, rules or regulations to the
2 contrary and pursuant to funds allocated to the Second Senatorial District from
3 poker fees, the Tinian and Aguiguan Legislative Delegation hereby appropriates
4 Forty Two Thousand Dollars ($42,000.00 USD) of the revenues collected for the
5 Second Senatorial District from the poker fees as follows:
6 (A) $42,000.00 allocated to the Mayor of Tinian and Aguiguan. $30,000.00
7 will be allocated for the operations of the Mayor's office and $12,000.00 for the
8 Mayor's discretionary fund.
9 SECTION 3. EXPENDITURE AUTHORITY.
10 The expenditure authority for funds under Section 2(A) shall be the Mayor
11 of Tinian and Aguiguan.
12 SECTION 4. REPROGRAMMING.
13 Funds appropriated under this act shall not be reprogrammed for any other
14 purpose and notwithstanding any provision of law, all funds appropriated under this
15 Act shall be without fiscal year limitation(s).
16 SECTION 5. REPORTING OF EXPENDITURES.
17 The recipient of the funds allocated under Section 2 of this Act shall be
18 responsible to report all expenses responsible to report all expenses to the Secretary
19 of the Department of Finance by the end of the 2025 calendar year. Provided further
20 that failure to provide all expenses, entities that were afforded funds under this Act
2
HOUSE LOCAL BILL No. 24- 2 r
1 shall not be able to avail to any future funds appropriated by the Tinian & Aguiguan
2 Legislative Delegation.
3 SECTION 6. SEVERABILITY.
4 If any provision of this Act or the application of any such provision to any
5 person or circumstance should be held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction,
6 the remainder of this Act or the application of its provisions to persons or
7 circumstances other than those to which it is held invalid shall not be affected
8 thereby.
9 SECTION 7. SAVINGS CLAUSE.
10 This Act and any repealer contained herein shall not be construed as
11 affecting any existing right acquired under contract or acquired under statutes
12 repealed or under any rule, regulation or order adopted under the statutes. Repealers
13 contained in this Act shall not affect any proceeding instituted under or pursuant to
14 prior law. The enactment of the Act shall not have the effect of terminating, or in
15 any way modifying, any liability, civil or criminal, which shall already be in
16 existence on the date this Act becomes effective.
17 SECTION 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.
18 This Act shall take effect upon its approval by the Governor or upon it
19 becoming law without such approval.

RE: Collection of Poker Fees on Tinian
Dear Mayor Aldan,
This is to inform you that on Friday, August 22, 2025, Huang Shun Corporation made a payment
to the Department of Finance on Tinian in the amount of $84,000 for the renewal of seven (7)
poker machine licenses. All payments have been deposited into the CNMI Treasury account on
August 22, 2025.
In accordance with Tinian Local Law 23-10, the annual local license fee for Tinian would be
$42,000.
Should you require additional information in regards to this matter, please do not hesitate to contact
me via email ma.mendiola@dof.gov.mp or (670) 433-1600.
Sincerely,
Milissa A. Mendiola
Resident Department Head
Department of Finance
