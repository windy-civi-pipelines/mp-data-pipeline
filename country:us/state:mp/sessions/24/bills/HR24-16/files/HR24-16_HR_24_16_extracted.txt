1 WHEREAS, the Legislature acknowledges that selfless service and
2 unwavering dedication to community upliftment, exemplified by those who
3 transition from serving in defense of freedom to championing wellness and
4 empowerment within our community, serve as a beacon of inspiration, igniting
5 within each of us a fervent desire to cultivate our passions and contribute
6 wholeheartedly to the flourishing of the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana
7 Islands (CNMI); and
8 WHEREAS, Project Buddy Check 670 was founded by veterans of the
9 CNMI, men and women from all branches of the United States Armed Forces, who
10 sought to preserve the camaraderie, accountability, and brotherhood that sustained
11 them during their military service; and
HOUSE RESOLUTION lq• t
1 WHEREAS, upon returning home, many of these veterans reconnected
2 through golf, which served both as recreation and therapy, and through these
3 gatherings, initiated the informal "buddy checks" that would evolve into a
4 movement dedicated to ensuring no veteran is left behind or suffers in silence; and
5 WHEREAS, the term "Buddy Check" originates from the U.S. Army,
6 referring to the practice of soldiers checking on their comrades to guarantee their
7 safety, readiness, and well-being, an act rooted in loyalty and trust that Project
8 Buddy Check 670 has carried forward into civilian life as a community-wide
9 mission of care, unity, and resilience; and
10 WHEREAS, in 2023, the founding veterans formalized Project Buddy
11 Check 670 into a structured organization committed to "Empowering Veterans and
12 Their Families in the CNMI," adopting bylaws, establishing a governing board, and
13 initiating the process for 501(c)(3)/(c)(19) nonprofit recognition; and
14 WHEREAS, since its inception, Project Buddy Check 670 has become one
15 of the most active and respected veteran organizations in the CNMI, leading
16 numerous initiatives including the Veterans Victory Classic Golf Tournament, the
17 Annual Veterans Day Family Fun Day, peer-support outreach programs, and
18 fostering collaborative partnerships with the CNMI Veterans Affairs Office, VA
19 Pacific Islands Healthcare System, Wounded Warrior Project, Veterans of Foreign
20 Wars (VFW), Tan Holdings, the Tan Siu Lin Foundation, and US Delegate
21 Congresswoman Kimberlyn King-Hinds' Office; and
