TINIAN AND AGUIGUAN LEGISLATIVE DELEGATION
SECOND SENATORIAL DISTRICT
TWENTY-FOURTH NORTHERN MARIANAS COMMONWEALTH LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION, 2025 H. L. B. 24-2, D1
A LOCAL APPROPRIATION BILL FOR AN ACT
FOR THE SECOND SENATORIAL DISTRICT
To appropriate Eighty Four Thousand Dollars ($84,000.00) from revenues collected
from poker fees in the Second Senatorial District; and for other purposes.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE SECOND SENATORIAL DISTRICT DELEGATION PURSUANT
TO CHAPTER 4, DIVISION 1, TITLE 1 OF THE COMMONWEALTH CODE:
1 SECTION 1. PURPOSE.
2 The purpose of this Act is to appropriate Eighty Four Thousand Dollars ($84,000.00 USD)
3 from revenues collected from poker fees in the Second Senatorial District. The certification of
4 funds was prepared on March 5, 2025 by the Resident Department Head of the Department of
5 Finance, Tinian. This appropriation of local funds is not part of the FY2025 Budget Act.
6 Therefore, this local appropriation of local funds does not contravene 1CMC §7401 and 1 CMC
7 §7404(d) or any other provision of the CNMI Budget Act.
8 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATIONS.
9 Notwithstanding any provision(s) of law, statutes, rules or regulations to the contrary and
10 pursuant to funds allocated to the Second Senatorial District from poker fees, the Tinian and
Tinian Local Law No. 24-02
HOUSE LOCAL BILL No. 24-2, DI
1 Aguiguan Legislative Delegation hereby appropriates Eighty Four Thousand Dollars ($84,000.00)
2 of the revenues collected for the Second Senatorial District from the poker fees as follows:
3 (A) $60,000.00 to the Mayor of Tinian and Aguiguan and the Tinian Municipal Treasurer
4 for personnel, operations, professional services, and public programs;
5 (B) $16,500 for the Mayor of Tinian and Aguiguan's discretionary fund; and
6 (C) $7,500.00 for the Tinian High School Valedictorian and Salutatorian Scholarships.
7 SECTION 3. EXPENDITURE AUTHORITY.
8 The expenditure authority for funds under Section 2(A)-(C) shall be the Mayor of Tinian
9 and Aguiguan.
10 SECTION 4. REPROGRAMMING.
11 Funds appropriated under this act shall not be reprogrammed for any other purpose and
12 notwithstanding any provision of law, all funds appropriated under this Act shall be without fiscal
13 year limitation(s).
14 SECTION 5. REPORTING OF EXPENDITURES.
15 The recipient of the funds allocated under Section 2 of this Act shall be responsible to
16 report all expenses responsible to report all expenses to the Secretary of the Department of Finance
17 by the end of the 2025 calendar year. Provided further that failure to provide all expenses, entities
18 that were afforded funds under this Act shall not be able to avail to any future funds appropriated
19 by the Tinian & Aguiguan Legislative Delegation.
20 SECTION 6. SEVERABILITY.
21 If any provision of this Act or the application of any such provision to any person or
2
Tinian Local Law No. 24-02
HOUSE LOCAL BILL No. 24-2, DI
1 circumstance should be held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remainder of this Act
2 or the application of its provisions to persons or circumstances other than those to which it is held
3 invalid shall not be affected thereby.
4 SECTION 7. SAVINGS CLAUSE.
5 This Act and any repealer contained herein shall not be construed as affecting any existing
6 right acquired under contract or acquired under statutes repealed or under any rule, regulation or
7 order adopted under the statutes. Repealers contained in this Act shall not affect any proceeding
8 instituted under or pursuant to prior law. The enactment of the Act shall not have the effect of
9 terminating, or in any way modifying, any liability, civil or criminal, which shall already be in
10 existence on the date this Act becomes effective.
11 SECTION 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.
12 This Act shall take effect upon its approval by the Governor or upon it becoming law
13 without such approval.
3

RE: Collection of Poker Fees on Tinian
Buenas Mayor Aldan,
I am writing to inform you that on February 25,2025, Huang Shun Corporation made payment to
the Department of Finance on Tinian in the amount of $36,000 for the renewal of three poker
machine licenses. On February 28,2025, Yong Chang Corporation renewed the poker machine
license for 11 machines and made a payment of $132,000. All payments were made at the
Tinian Department of Finance and funds have been deposited into the CNMI Treasury account.
In accordance with Tinian Local Law 23-10, the annual local license fee for Tinian would be
$84,000.
Should you need additional information, please do not hesitate to contact me at
ma.mendiolaadoEgov.mp or (670) 433-1600/04.
Milissa A. Mendiola
Resident Department Head
Department of Finance
