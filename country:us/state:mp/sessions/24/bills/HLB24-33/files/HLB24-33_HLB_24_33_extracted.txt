Section 1:
TWENTY-FOURTH NORTHERN MARIANAS COMMONWEALTH
LEGISLATURE
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
SESSION, 2025 H. L. B. 24- 13
FOR THE THIRD SENATORIAL DISTRICT
To amend Saipan Local Law 24-05 that appropriated the remaining fund
balance of $85,500 under Saipan Local Law 23-04 for the repair,
maintenance and improvements of recreational facilities in Precinct IV;
and for other purposes.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE THIRD SENATORIAL DISTRICT
DELEGATION PURSUANT TO CHAPTER 4, DIVISION 1, TITLE 1 OF
THE COMMONWEALTH CODE:
1 SECTION 1. PURPOSE.
2 The purpose of this Act is to amend Saipan Local Law 24-05 to reflect the
3 accurate amount reflected in the fund certification in the amount of $84,500 as
4 provided by the Department of Finance and attached for reference. In addition, the
5 SNILD Delegation passed House Local Bill 24-8, in its original form, on First and
6 Final Reading on June 18, 2025, however, the language contained in the transmitted
7 version was not identical to the passed version. Therefore, the intent of this Act is
8 the update the certified amount available for appropriation and amend the language
9 to reflect to true intent of House Local Bill 24-8.
HOUSE LOCAL BILL 24-3
1 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT.
2 The amount of $85,500.00 reflected in Saipan Local Law 24-5 specifically
3 in the Title; Section 1 and Section 2 shall be amended to read $84,500.00
4 SECTION 3. AMENDMENT. Section 3 and Section 4 of Saipan Local Law 24-
5 05 is hereby amended to read as follows:
6 "SECTION 3. EXPENDITURE AUTHORITY
7 The expenditure authority for the funds under Section 2(a) shall be the
8 Director of Parks and Recreation in consultation with the Secreta ' of the
9 Department of Lands and Natural Resources and the expenditure authority for the
10 funds under Section 2(b) shall be the Director of Roads and Grounds in consultation
11 with the Secretary of the Department of Public Works.
12 SECTION 4. REPROGRAMMING.
13 The expenditure authority for the funds under Section 2(A) shall be the
14 President of NSA in direct collaboration with the Director of Parks and Recreation.
15 SECTION 4. REPROGRAMMING.
16 Notwithstanding any laws, provisions, or regulations to the contrary, funds
17 appropriated under this act shall not be reprogrammed for any other purpose."
18 SECTION 4. SEVERABILITY.
19 If any provision of this Act or the application of any such provision to any
20 person or circumstance should be held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction,
21 the remainder of this Act or the application of its provisions to persons or
2
HOUSE LOCAL BILL 24- 43
1 circumstances other than those to which it is held invalid shall not be affected
2 thereby.
3 SECTION 5. SAVINGS CLAUSE.
4 This Act and any repealer contained herein shall not be construed as
5 affecting any existing right acquired under contract or acquired under statutes
6 repealed or under any rule, regulation or order adopted under the statutes. Repealers
7 contained in this Act shall not affect any proceeding instituted under or pursuant to
8 prior law. The enactment of the Act shall not have the effect of terminating, or in
9 any way modifying, any liability, civil or criminal, which shall already be in
10 existence on the date this Act becomes effective.
11 SECTION 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.
12 This Act shall take effect upon its approval by the Governor or it becoming
13 law without such approval.
