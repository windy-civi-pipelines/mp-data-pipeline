Section 1:
TWENTY-FOURTH NORTHERN MARIANAS COMMONWEALTH
LEGISLATURE
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
21
SESSION, 2025 H. L. B. 24-
FOR THE FIRST SENATORIAL DISTRICT
To appropriate Three Hundred and Five Thousand Dollars
($305,000.00) for the continued operation and regulatory oversight
of the Rota Casino Gaming Commission; and for other purposes.
BE IT ENACTED BY I HE FIRST SENATORIAL DISTRICT
DELEGATION PURSUANT TO CHAPTER 4, DIVISION 1, TITLE 1 OF
THE COMMONWEALTH CODE:
1 SECTION 1. PURPOSE.
2 The purpose of this bill is to appropriate local funds for Three Hundred and
3 Five Thousand Dollars ($305,000.00) for the continued operation and regulatory
4 oversight of the Rota Casino Gaming Commission and other needs of the
5 municipality. These funds were received by the Municipality of Rota pursuant to
6 10 CMC § 12141. This local appropriation is made under the NMI CONST. art. VI
7 § 8, cl. 2; 1 CMC §§ 1402-03, and 1408; and 10 CMC § 12103. This appropriation
8 is based on the budget request adopted and approved by the 20th Rota Municipal
9 Council during its 6th Regular Session, held on August 28, 2025.
ROUSE LOCAL BILL
24-2?)
1 SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION.
2 Notwithstanding any provisions, statutes, laws or regulations to the
3 contrary, the Rota Legislative Delegation hereby appropriates, from the funds
4 received by the Municipality of Rota pursuant to 10 CMC § 12141, Three Hundred
5 and Five Thousand Dollars ($305,000.00) to the Rota Casino Gaming Commission
6 (RCGC) for its operations and other needs of the municipality to be used as follows:
7 (a) $292,000 for the RCGC Operations; and
8 (b) $13,000 for the payment of salaries of the previous RCGC members.
9 SECTION 3. EXPENDITURE AUTHORITY.
10 The RCGC Chairperson shall be the expenditure authority of the funds
11 under Section 2. Any compensation owed to a former Commission Member shall
12 be paid in accordance with 4 CMC § 9711.
13 SECTION 4. REPROGRAMMING.
14 Notwithstanding any laws, provisions, or regulations to the contrary, funds
15 appropriated under this Act shall not be reprogrammed for any other purpose unless
16 a written justification is submitted to the RLD Chairperson for approval. Three
17 RLD members shall sign the approval of the requested reprogramming.
18 SECTION 5. REPORTING.
19 The expenditure authority shall submit detailed monthly reports showing
20 the expenditure of funds appropriated under this Act. These reports shall be
21 furnished to the Chairpersons of the Rota Municipal Council and the First
2
HOUSE LOCAL BILL 24- Al
1 Senatorial Delegation until all appropriated funds have been fully expended. If the
2 expenditure authority fails to meet this reporting requirement, no further
3 expenditures shall be authorized under this Act until the required reports are
4 submitted.
5 SECTION 6. SEVERABILITY.
6 If any provision of this Act or the application of any such provision to any
7 person or circumstance should be held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction,
8 the remainder of this Act or the application of its provisions to persons or
9 circumstances other than those to which it is held invalid shall not be affected
10 thereby.
11 SECTION 7. SAVINGS CLAUSE.
12 This Act and any repealer contained herein shall not be construed as
13 affecting any existing right acquired under contract or acquired under statutes
14 repealed or under any rule, regulation, or order adopted under the statutes.
15 Repealers contained in this Act shall not affect any proceeding instituted under or
16 pursuant to prior law. The enactment of the Act shall not have the effect of
17 terminating, or in any way modifying, any liability, civil or criminal, which shall
18 already be in existence on the date this Act becomes effective.
19 SECTION 8. EFFECTIVE DATE.
20 This Act shall take effect upon its approval by the Governor or it becoming law
21 without such approval.
